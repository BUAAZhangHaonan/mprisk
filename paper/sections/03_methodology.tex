\section{Methodology}

\subsection{Task Setup}

Samples are grouped as Conflict, Ambiguous, or Aligned.
The main experiments focus on Conflict and Aligned samples.
For each protocol, we construct \(M_1\), \(M_2\), and \(M_{12}\) conditions.

\subsection{Pre-generation State Extraction at \(t_0\)}

\(t_0\) is the last conditioning state before the first generated token.
For each of the \(P=8\) registered equivalent prompts, we extract the complete prefill
layer trajectory for \(M_1\) (V), \(M_2\) (T/A), and \(M_{12}\) (joint input).
No generated token or post-generation response is used by the state representation.

\subsection{Trajectory Manifold Encoder}

Let \(h_{c,p}^{(\ell)}\) be the hidden state at layer \(\ell\), condition
\(c\in\{1,2,12\}\), and prompt \(p\). TME first applies per-layer
\(\ell_2\) normalization, then sends the ordered layer sequence through one shared
GRU for all three conditions:
\begin{equation}
  \tilde h_{c,p}^{(\ell)} =
  \frac{h_{c,p}^{(\ell)}}{\lVert h_{c,p}^{(\ell)}\rVert_2},
  \qquad
  z_{c,p}=\operatorname{norm}
  \!\left(\operatorname{GRU}_{\theta}
  (\tilde h_{c,p}^{(1:L)})\right).
\end{equation}
Thus every condition embedding lies on the unit hypersphere. The ordered relation
features and sample-level relation representation are
\begin{align}
  u_p &= [1-z_{1,p}^{\top}z_{2,p},\;
           1-z_{12,p}^{\top}z_{1,p},\;
           1-z_{12,p}^{\top}z_{2,p}], \\
  r_p &= \operatorname{norm}(Wu_p+b).
\end{align}
Conflict/Aligned supervision is attached only to this three-condition relation; it is
never copied onto an individual condition trajectory. TME uses exactly one standard
Proxy Anchor loss with one proxy per class. It does not use an auxiliary classifier,
SupCon, or prompt-consistency loss. During training, one registered prompt is selected
per sample and epoch; validation and testing aggregate all eight prompts. After fitting,
the encoder is frozen and exports condition-level \(z\) and sample-level \(r\).

For comparison, Single-Point concatenates the final-layer states of the three conditions
and applies an ordinary linear Conflict/Aligned classifier. Trajectory MLP consumes the
full three-condition layer trajectories and applies an ordinary MLP classifier. Neither
baseline inherits TME's spherical relation module or Proxy Anchor objective.

\subsection{State Measures: \(S\), \(D\), and \(R\)}

We use the geodesic distance
\(d_g(a,b)=\arccos(\operatorname{clip}(a^{\top}b,-1,1))\). For each condition,
\begin{align}
  \mu_c &= \operatorname{norm}\!\left(\sum_{p=1}^{P}z_{c,p}\right), \\
  s_c &= \frac{1}{P}\sum_{p=1}^{P}d_g^2(z_{c,p},\mu_c).
\end{align}
State Dispersion, Modality Split, and signed Joint Lean are then
\begin{align}
  S &= (s_1+s_2+s_{12})/3, \\
  D &= \frac{d_g(\mu_1,\mu_2)}{\sqrt{s_1+s_2}+\varepsilon}, \\
  R &= \frac{d_g(\mu_{12},\mu_2)-d_g(\mu_{12},\mu_1)}
             {d_g(\mu_1,\mu_2)+\varepsilon}.
\end{align}
Our sign convention is fixed: \(R>0\) denotes V-lean and \(R<0\) denotes
T/A-lean. Direction is interpreted primarily when \(D>\tau\).

The thresholds \(\kappa\) and \(\tau\) are the registered 95th percentiles of
\(S\) and stable-sample \(D\), respectively, estimated only on an independent
Aligned calibration split. The sample-specific uncertainty band
\(\delta_i=1.96\,\operatorname{SE}_{\mathrm{boot}}(R_i)\) is obtained by
synchronous prompt resampling.

\subsection{Four State Patterns and State-guided Response}

The hierarchy is fixed: \(S>\kappa\) gives Confusion; otherwise
\(D\leq\tau\) gives Consensus; otherwise \(|R|\leq\delta_i\) gives
Balanced; all remaining samples are Dominant. High-dispersion samples therefore remain
in the analysis as Confusion and are not forced into the \(D\)--\(R\) geometry.
The same state labels can be used to route a lightweight response policy in Stage 2.
